1 · Provider Gets Verified
Registers & uploads docs
Reviews & approves provider
Verified — ready to take jobs
2 · Customer Creates a Job
Describes problem & sets urgency
Broadcasts to nearby providers
Receives alert & submits quote
Reviews quotes & confirms provider
Holds visiting fee in escrow
Job confirmed — heads to site
3 · Provider Does the Work
Travels to customer location
Sends live ETA updates
Tracks provider arrival
Completes job on site
4 · Payment & Reviews
Sends invoice to customer
Pays invoice in-app
Releases payment to provider
Payout received
Leaves rating & review
5 · Dispute (if any)
Raises dispute with evidence
Reviews both sides & decides
Processes refund if approved
Dispute resolved
6 · Platform Rules
Sets commission & dispatch rules
Applies rules to every job

❓ Questions & Edge Cases
Cancellation window & refund rules (confirmed): • Before provider accepts → free cancel, nothing charged. • Provider accepted but not yet left → full visit fee refunded to customer. • Provider en route (left for job) → visit fee split 50/50 between platform and provider. • Provider arrived → full visit fee goes to provider, no refund. • Cancellation reason is required from the customer.
OPEN — No visit fee scenario: if a service has no visit fee and the provider is already en route or has arrived, should cancellation be disabled after a buffer time (e.g. 5–10 min after provider accepts)? What should that buffer window be?
OPEN — Provider cancellation window: what is the time limit within which a provider can cancel after accepting a job? After that window, cancellation should be restricted or penalised.
Account flag: after 3 cancellations, the account is flagged for admin review. Confirm the rolling window (e.g. 3 in 30 days).
Does the provider receive any compensation when a customer cancels after the provider has already left? (Proposed: provider receives 50% of visit fee in that scenario.)
OPEN — If there is no visit fee, what compensation (if any) should the provider receive for a cancellation after they have already left for the job?
